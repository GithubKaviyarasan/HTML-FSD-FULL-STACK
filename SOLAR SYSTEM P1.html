SOLAR SYSTEM

The Solar System is a gravitationally bound system formed 4.6 billion years ago, comprising the Sun and celestial objects orbiting it, including eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. Located in the Milky Way's Orion Arm, the Sun holds 99.85% of the system’s mass.The SunType: A G2V yellow dwarf star, constituting nearly all the system's mass.Composition: Primarily hydrogen and helium, generating energy through nuclear fusion.Location: Orbits the center of the Milky Way galaxy, taking roughly 230 million years to complete one rotation.The Eight Planets (In order from the Sun)Mercury: lest planet, closest to the sun, no moons.Venus: Hottest planet due to thick atmosphere, rotates backwards, no moons.Earth: Only known planet with life, rocky with significant water.Mars: Known as the Red Planet, home to the largest volcano, Olympus Mons.Jupiter: Largest planet, a gas giant with 60+ moons.Saturn: Known for its prominent, complex ring system.Uranus: An ice giant that rotates on its side.Neptune: The most distant, coldest planet, and a blue gas giant.Key ComponentsTerrestrial Planets: The four inner, rocky planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars).Gas Giants: The four outer, large planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune).Dwarf Planets: Small planets like Pluto, Eris, and Ceres, located mostly in the Kuiper Belt.Asteroid Belt: A region between Mars and Jupiter filled with rocky bodies. Kuiper Belt/Oort Cloud: Distant, icy regions beyond Neptune that are sources of comets

SUN

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, appearing as a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma (mostly hydrogen and helium) that provides heat, light, and energy for Earth. Formed about 4.6 billion years ago, it holds over99% of the solar system's mass and its gravity keeps all planets in orbit. Key Facts About the Sun:Distance: Located about 93 million miles (150 million km) from Earth, < taking light about 8 minutes to travel between them.Size: Its radius is approximately 695,700 km, making it about 109 times wider than Earth.Energy Source: The core (15 million °C) generates energy through nuclear fusion, turning hydrogen into helium.Surface: The visible surface is called the photosphere, with temperatures around 5,500°C.Rotation: It rotates faster at its equator (25 Earth days) than at its poles (36 Earth days).
